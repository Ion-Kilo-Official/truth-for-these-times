Introduction: Signs You Can't Ignore

This study guide accompanies the sermon "Signs You Can't Ignore," which explores what the Bible reveals about the science of the end of the world and the climatic event of Christ's Second Coming. The sermon begins by recalling the 1938 Orson Welles radio drama, "War of the Worlds," illustrating how a message, even if fictitious, can grip people with fear or conviction based on its presentation. Today, similar anxieties about the world's end arise from concerns like climate change, nuclear war, pandemics, and resource depletion.

The speaker emphasizes that despite these fears, God is in control and has not left us in the dark. He has provided signs and warnings in the Bible so that we can be aware and prepared. This world, with its pain and suffering, must end for a better world to come.

Key Question from Sermon: How can we understand the Bible's predictions about the end of the world and be prepared for Christ's return?

1. Jesus' Prophecy: The Destruction of Jerusalem - A Foreshadowing

Jesus made a startling prediction about the magnificent temple in Jerusalem, stating that "not one stone shall be left here upon another." This shocked His disciples, leading them to ask about the timing of these events and the signs of His coming and the end of the age. Jesus was actually speaking of two distinct events: the destruction of Jerusalem and His Second Coming.

Regarding Jerusalem, Jesus warned of the "abomination of desolation" and urged those in Judea to flee when they saw armies surrounding the city. This prophecy was strikingly fulfilled around AD 70 when the Roman armies, after an initial withdrawal that allowed believers to escape, returned and destroyed Jerusalem and the temple. Over a million people are believed to have perished.

Key Scriptures:

  • Matthew 24:2:
    "Do you not see all these things? Assuredly, I say to you, not one stone shall be left here upon another, that shall not be thrown down."
  • Matthew 24:3:
    "Now as He sat on the Mount of Olives, the disciples came to Him privately, saying, 'Tell us, when will these things be? And what will be the sign of Your coming, and of the end of the age?'"
  • Matthew 24:15-16:
    "Therefore when you see the ‚Äòabomination of desolation,‚Äô spoken of by Daniel the prophet, standing in the holy place (whoever reads, let him understand), then let those who are in Judea flee to the mountains."
  • Luke 21:20 (referenced via Luke's account):
    "But when you see Jerusalem surrounded by armies, then know that its desolation is near."
Key Takeaway:The accurate fulfillment of Jesus' prophecy concerning Jerusalem's destruction demonstrates God's foreknowledge and the reliability of His Word, urging us to heed His warnings about future events, including His Second Coming.

Reflection:

  • Why was the destruction of Jerusalem such a significant and shocking event for Jesus' disciples and early Christians?
  • The sermon highlights that those who believed Christ's prophecy and watched for the signs were saved from Jerusalem's destruction. What lessons can we learn today for our preparedness for Christ's Second Coming?

2. Signs of the Times: Prelude to the Second Coming (Part 1)

Jesus provided clear signs that would precede His Second Coming and the end of the world, enabling His followers to know when the time is near. These signs include widespread global issues and natural phenomena.

Signs Highlighted:

  • Political Strife and Wars: Jesus said, "For nation will rise against nation, and kingdom against kingdom." The 20th century was noted as the bloodiest in history, and violence, hatred, and bloodshed continue to escalate.
  • Famines: "And there will be famines..." Despite advancements, millions die from starvation annually, and many more are malnourished as the world population grows.
  • Pestilences (Diseases): "...pestilences..." Modern medicine notwithstanding, diseases like AIDS, malaria, Ebola, COVID-19, and various flus have killed millions. Lifestyle diseases and those with unknown causes or cures also pose significant threats.
  • Environmental Deterioration: The prophet Isaiah spoke of the heavens vanishing like smoke and the earth growing old like a garment. Air and water pollution, along with increasingly devastating natural disasters, affirm this prediction.

Key Scriptures:

  • Matthew 24:7:
    "For nation will rise against nation, and kingdom against kingdom. And there will be famines, pestilences, and earthquakes in various places."
  • Isaiah 51:6 (paraphrased in sermon):
    "Lift up your eyes to the heavens, And look on the earth beneath. For the heavens will vanish away like smoke, The earth will grow old like a garment, And those who dwell in it will die in like manner; But My salvation will be forever, And My righteousness will not be abolished."
Key Takeaway:Jesus forewarned of increasing global turmoil‚Äîwars, famines, diseases, and environmental crises‚Äîas indicators that the end of the age is drawing near.

Reflection:

  • How do current global events and reports align with these predicted signs discussed in the sermon?
  • In what ways can awareness of these signs impact a believer's perspective on world events and their personal priorities?

3. Signs of the Times: Prelude to the Second Coming (Part 2)

The biblical prophecies continue with more signs pointing towards the end of the age, including dramatic natural events and a significant decline in societal morality.

Further Signs:

  • Earthquakes and Heavenly Signs: Jesus predicted "earthquakes in various places," and the sermon notes their increasing frequency and destructive power, citing the 2004 tsunami as an example. Luke's Gospel adds "great earthquakes... fearful sights and great signs from heaven," and "distress of nations, with perplexity, the sea and the waves roaring."
  • Deteriorating Morality: Jesus compared the conditions of the last days to those of Sodom and Gomorrah. The Apostle Paul in 2 Timothy described people as "lovers of themselves, lovers of money, boasters, proud, blasphemers, disobedient to parents, unthankful, unholy, unloving, unforgiving, slanderers, without self-control, brutal, despisers of good, traitors, headstrong, haughty, lovers of pleasure rather than lovers of God, having a form of godliness but denying its power."

Key Scriptures:

  • Luke 21:11 (referenced via Luke's account):
    "And there will be great earthquakes in various places, and famines and pestilences; and there will be fearful sights and great signs from heaven."
  • Luke 21:25 (referenced via Luke's account):
    "And there will be signs in the sun, in the moon, and in the stars; and on the earth distress of nations, with perplexity, the sea and the waves roaring."
  • Jude 1:7 (referenced regarding Sodom and Gomorrah):
    "...as Sodom and Gomorrah, and the cities around them in a similar manner to these, having given themselves over to sexual immorality and gone after strange flesh, are set forth as an example, suffering the vengeance of eternal fire."
  • 2 Timothy 3:1-5:
    "But know this, that in the last days perilous times will come: For men will be lovers of themselves, lovers of money, boasters, proud, blasphemers, disobedient to parents, unthankful, unholy, unloving, unforgiving, slanderers, without self-control, brutal, despisers of good, traitors, headstrong, haughty, lovers of pleasure rather than lovers of God, having a form of godliness but denying its power."
Key Takeaway:A marked increase in natural disasters and heavenly phenomena, coupled with a significant decline in societal morality and ethics, are prophesied as clear markers of the approaching end times.

  • How does the moral climate described in 2 Timothy 3 compare to what you observe in contemporary society? The speaker mentioned a preacher suggesting God should apologize to Sodom and Gomorrah. What does this stark comment imply about modern wickedness?

5. Like the Days of Noah: A World Unprepared

Jesus drew a powerful parallel between the conditions of the world before His Second Coming and the days of Noah before the flood. This comparison highlights a prevalent spiritual apathy and preoccupation with worldly affairs.

In Noah's time, people were engrossed in their daily routines‚Äî"eating and drinking, marrying and giving in marriage"‚Äîwith little regard for spiritual matters or Noah's warnings, until the flood came and swept them away. The sermon emphasizes that this doesn't mean daily activities are wrong, but losing sight of God amidst them is the danger.

Similarly, before Jesus' first coming, many of His own people were not ready and did not receive Him because they had ignored the signs. The warning is clear: we must not repeat this mistake concerning His Second Coming.

Key Scripture:

  • Matthew 24:37-39:
    "But as the days of Noah were, so also will the coming of the Son of Man be. For as in the days before the flood, they were eating and drinking, marrying and giving in marriage, until the day that Noah entered the ark, and did not know until the flood came and took them all away, so also will the coming of the Son of Man be."
Key Takeaway:Just as in Noah's time, a significant portion of the world will likely be caught unprepared by Christ's return due to spiritual indifference and an overemphasis on worldly concerns, neglecting clear warnings.

Reflection:

  • In what specific ways does modern society mirror the "days of Noah" as described in the sermon and scripture?
  • The sermon mentions that "it's wrong to lose sight of God" in our daily lives. What practical steps can individuals take to maintain spiritual focus and preparedness amidst everyday responsibilities and distractions?

6. The Urgent Call: Be Ready Today!

The culmination of all these signs and warnings is an urgent call to readiness. God's warnings are not meant to frighten without purpose, but are expressions of His love, desiring that all should be saved and spend eternity with Him.

Jesus said, "When you see all these things, know that it is near‚Äîat the doors!" The sermon uses the powerful analogy of the steamship Central America. Its captain, despite distress signals and offers of immediate help, chose to wait until morning, resulting in the ship sinking and nearly all on board perishing. The lesson is stark: delaying a decision for Christ, or for spiritual preparedness, can have eternal consequences.

The Bible emphatically states, "Today is the day of salvation." There is no guarantee of tomorrow. The call is to make a decision for Christ right now, to ask for His help to prepare for His soon return.

Key Scriptures:

  • Matthew 24:33 (referenced):
    "So you also, when you see all these things, know that it is near‚Äîat the doors!"
  • Implied (related to "Today is the day of salvation"): 2 Corinthians 6:2:
    "For He says: 'In an acceptable time I have heard you, And in the day of salvation I have helped you.' Behold, now is the accepted time; behold, now is the day of salvation."
Key Takeaway:The abundance of fulfilled and unfolding signs of Christ's return underscores the Bible's urgent call for immediate repentance, faith, and acceptance of salvation, as procrastination in spiritual matters can lead to eternal loss.

Reflection:

  • The story of the Central America captain illustrates the danger of delay. What "dangers" or spiritual needs might people be tempted to postpone addressing in their lives today?
  • The sermon ends with a prayer and a call to "give [God] permission to save us from our sins." What does it practically mean to "make your decision for Christ right now" and allow Him to save you?

Conclusion: Heeding the Signs, Embracing Hope in Christ

The sermon powerfully articulates that God, in His great love, has not left humanity uninformed about the future. He has provided numerous signs, recorded in His Word, that point to the culmination of this age and the glorious return of Jesus Christ. These signs‚Äîranging from political upheavals and natural disasters to moral decay and spiritual deception, alongside the global spread of the Gospel‚Äîare becoming increasingly evident.

The central message urges each listener not to be like those who ignored the warnings in Noah's day or at Jerusalem's destruction, nor like the captain who delayed action until it was too late. Instead, the call is to recognize the lateness of the hour, to turn to Christ today, and to allow Him to prepare us for His kingdom.

Final Thought:

As the signs of the times unfold around us, will you choose to heed God's warnings, fortify your mind with His Word, and make a heartfelt decision for Christ today, ensuring you are ready for His soon return?
