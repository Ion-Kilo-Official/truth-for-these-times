How to Use This Study Guide

This guide is designed to help you delve deeper into the biblical principles concerning God's law and morality, alongside the sermon. As you go through each section:

  • Consider listening to the accompanying podcast audio using the player above for a richer experience.
  • Read the summary points and key scriptures.

Introduction: The Unexpected Criminal

A man, outraged by a burglary at his home, rushed to the police station demanding justice. While sifting through mugshots to identify the culprit, an officer paused, looked closely at a picture, and then at the man. "That's you," the officer declared, "and there's an outstanding warrant for your arrest." The very individual demanding justice was, ironically, a wanted criminal himself.

This story highlights a critical question: In a world where lines blur, what truly defines right and wrong? This study explores this question, seeking answers from the timeless wisdom of the Bible.

A World in Moral Crisis

Crime and violence seem to be escalating, appearing in unexpected places ‚Äì workplaces, schools, and even churches. Society grapples with self-centered role models, a casual attitude towards dishonesty, and a general disrespect for traditional values, God, and family.

Even within religious circles, confusion about morality persists. Some teach that God's commandments are abolished or irrelevant. The consequence? A society reaping broken homes, uncontrolled behavior, and rampant crime, fulfilling the prophet Hosea's words:

"They sow the wind, And reap the whirlwind." (Hosea 8:7, NKJV)

Reflection:

  • In what ways do you see a moral decline in society today?
  • How might the church, at times, contribute to confusion about right and wrong?

God's Unchanging Standard

Who determines what is right or wrong? Human judgment is often flawed. The Bible warns:

"There is a way that seems right to a man, But its end is the way of death." (Proverbs 14:12, NKJV)

Our natural inclinations may not align with truth, especially if truth feels inconvenient. The Apostle Paul foresaw a time when people would reject sound teaching for messages that suit their own desires:

"For the time will come when they will not endure sound doctrine, but according to their own desires, because they have itching ears, they will heap up for themselves teachers; and they will turn their ears away from the truth, and be turned aside to fables." (2 Timothy 4:3-4, NKJV)

True freedom isn't found by discarding rules, but by adhering to the divine standard God established for a prosperous and free society: His Law.

Key Takeaway: Human standards of right and wrong are unreliable; God's Law provides the true and unchanging foundation for morality.

The Ten Commandments: God's Blueprint for Life

At Mount Sinai, after freeing the Israelites from bondage, God presented them with His Ten Commandments. These are not arbitrary rules but principles flowing from His character of love, guiding our relationship with Him and with others.

"I am the LORD your God, who brought you out of the land of Egypt, out of the house of bondage." (Exodus 20:2, NKJV)

A Summary of the Ten Commandments (Exodus 20:3-17):

  • 1. You shall have no other gods before Me.
  • 2. You shall not make for yourself a carved image... you shall not bow down to them.
  • 3. You shall not take the name of the LORD your God in vain.
  • 4. Remember the Sabbath day, to keep it holy.
  • 5. Honor your father and your mother.
  • 6. You shall not murder.
  • 7. You shall not commit adultery.
  • 8. You shall not steal.
  • 9. You shall not bear false witness against your neighbor.
  • 10. You shall not covet anything that is your neighbor's.
"When the LORD had finished speaking with him on Mount Sinai, He gave Moses the two tablets of the testimony, tablets of stone, written by the finger of God." (Exodus 31:18, CSB)

Reflection:

  • Why do you think God wrote these commandments on stone?
  • How do these commandments reflect God's love and concern for humanity?

The Law's Eternal Nature

God's law, an expression of His unselfish love, existed from eternity, governing even the angels in heaven. The rebellion led by Satan was against this divine order.

"And war broke out in heaven: Michael and his angels fought with the dragon... So the great dragon was cast out, that serpent of old, called the Devil and Satan, who deceives the whole world; he was cast to the earth, and his angels were cast out with him." (Revelation 12:7,9, NKJV)

The loyal angels continue to obey His word (Psalm 103:20). Adam and Eve's sin was a transgression of this pre-existing law, for "where there is no law there is no transgression" (Romans 4:15). Abraham, centuries before Sinai, obeyed God's commandments (Genesis 26:5). Joseph recognized adultery as a "great wickedness and sin against God" (Genesis 39:9). Even before the formal giving of the law at Sinai, God rebuked the Israelites for breaking His laws concerning the Sabbath (Exodus 16:28).

Key Takeaway: God's law is not a temporary construct but an eternal principle, reflecting His unchanging character and operative since creation.

The Law and Love

The Ten Commandments are fundamentally about love: love for God (first four) and love for fellow humans (last six). Jesus summarized this:

"‚ÄòYou shall love the Lord your God with all your heart, with all your soul, and with all your mind.‚Äô This is the first and great commandment. And the second is like it: ‚ÄòYou shall love your neighbor as yourself.‚Äô On these two commandments hang all the Law and the Prophets." (Matthew 22:37-40, NKJV)

Jesus also directly linked love and obedience:

"If you love Me, keep My commandments." (John 14:15, NKJV)

Reflection:

  • How does understanding the law as an expression of love change your perspective on obedience?
  • Pick one of the Ten Commandments. How does it demonstrate love for God or love for others?

Purpose and Perfection of the Law

God's law, concise yet comprehensive, is perfect and intended to bring peace, happiness, and protection.

"The law of the LORD is perfect, converting the soul." (Psalm 19:7, NKJV)

Because it reflects God's perfect, unchanging character, the law itself is unalterable.

"And it is easier for heaven and earth to pass away than for one tittle of the law to fail." (Luke 16:17, NKJV, cf. Matthew 5:18)

It is not a fence to keep us from happiness, but a protective wall. God desires our well-being through obedience:

"Oh, that they had such a heart in them that they would fear Me and always keep all My commandments, that it might be well with them and with their children forever!" (Deuteronomy 5:29, NKJV)
Key Takeaway: The law is a perfect, unchanging expression of God's love, designed for our protection and well-being.

The Law as a Mirror

Like a mirror, God's law reveals our true condition ‚Äì our sinfulness. It doesn't change our condition, but it makes us aware of it.

"For by the law is the knowledge of sin." (Romans 3:20, NKJV)

Paul personally experienced this:

"I would not have known sin except through the law. For I would not have known covetousness unless the law had said, ‚ÄòYou shall not covet.‚Äô" (Romans 7:7, NKJV)

The primary spiritual function of the law is to point out our sin and, consequently, our desperate need for a Savior.

Reflection:

  • If the law is like a mirror, what "blemishes" might it reveal in our lives today?
  • Why is it important to see our sinfulness before we can appreciate the Savior?

Why the Law Cannot Save

While the law reveals sin, it cannot provide the solution. It cannot grant power to overcome sin or erase past guilt. Even perfect future obedience cannot atone for past transgressions.

From Eden, God instituted sacrificial offerings (an innocent lamb) to illustrate the deadly consequence of sin and to point forward to the ultimate sacrifice: Jesus Christ, the Lamb of God, who would die for humanity's sins.

"For if there had been a law given which could have given life, truly righteousness would have been by the law." (Galatians 3:21, NKJV)
Key Takeaway: The law diagnoses sin but cannot cure it; salvation comes only through Christ's sacrifice, not by law-keeping.

The Cross Upholds the Law

Jesus' death on Calvary does not abolish the law. Instead, it magnifies the law's importance. If the law could have been changed or set aside, Christ's death would have been unnecessary.

Consider this: If there is no law, there is no sin (transgression of the law). If there is no sin, there is no need for grace (God's unmerited favor). If there is no need for grace, there is no need for a Savior or the Cross.

The Cross is an eternal memorial to the price God paid because His law is unchangeable and humanity was guilty. God, in His love, found a way to save sinners in harmony with His holy principles.

"For God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have everlasting life." (John 3:16, NKJV)

Reflection:

  • How does Christ's death demonstrate the unchanging nature of God's law?
  • If someone says, "Now that Christ has died, we don't need to worry about the law," how would you respond based on this section?

Saved by Grace, Not Works

Our salvation is a gift, purchased by Christ's blood. Nothing we do can add to His sacrifice or make us worthy.

"For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord." (Romans 6:23, NKJV)
"For by grace you have been saved through faith, and that not of yourselves; it is the gift of God, not of works, lest anyone should boast." (Ephesians 2:8-9, NKJV)
Key Takeaway: Salvation is entirely by God's grace, received through faith, not earned by obedience or good works.

Grace, Obedience, and a New Heart

If we are not saved by works, does this mean we are free to disobey God's law? Paul answers emphatically:

"What shall we say then? Shall we continue in sin that grace may abound? Certainly not! How shall we who died to sin live any longer in it?" (Romans 6:1-2, NKJV)

Those who receive salvation experience a change of heart. They desire to live in harmony with God's law of love. Christ not only forgives but also provides the power to obey, promising:

"I will put My laws in their mind and write them on their hearts." (Hebrews 8:10, NKJV, cf. Jeremiah 31:33)

Obedience becomes a love response, not a means to earn salvation. Just as a pardoned prisoner wouldn't continue a life of crime, a pardoned sinner desires to honor the law.

Reflection:

  • What is the difference between trying to obey God to be saved, and obeying God because you are saved?
  • How does the promise of God writing His law on our hearts offer hope for true obedience?

Following Christ's Example

Jesus Himself lived in perfect obedience to His Father's commandments, driven by love.

"I have kept My Father‚Äôs commandments and abide in His love." (John 15:10, NKJV)

Our motivation for keeping His commandments is also love:

"If you love Me, keep My commandments." (John 14:15, NKJV)
Key Takeaway: Love is the supreme motivation for obedience, following the example of Christ.

God's People in the End Times

The book of Revelation describes God's faithful people in the last days as those who keep His commandments through faith in Jesus.

"Here is the patience of the saints; here are those who keep the commandments of God and the faith of Jesus." (Revelation 14:12, NKJV)

This obedience will not always be easy or popular, as Satan opposes those who live in harmony with God's character.

"And the dragon was enraged with the woman, and he went to make war with the rest of her offspring, who keep the commandments of God and have the testimony of Jesus Christ." (Revelation 12:17, NKJV)

Reflection:

  • Why is there a connection between keeping God's commandments and having "the faith of Jesus"?
  • What kind of opposition might those who strive to keep God's commandments face today?

The Ultimate Act of Obedience

The greatest demonstration of love and obedience was Christ's prayer in Gethsemane, choosing the Father's will over His own, even unto death.

"O My Father, if it is possible, let this cup pass from Me; nevertheless, not as I will, but as You will." (Matthew 26:39, NKJV)

He died the death that was ours so we could live eternally with Him, all because He loved us. This profound love awakens a responsive love in our hearts.

Key Takeaway: Christ's selfless obedience, rooted in love, is the ultimate inspiration for our own commitment to God's will.

Conclusion: A Heart Transformed by Love

God's people love their Redeemer deeply and desire to do His will, living by His principles. His simple request echoes: "If you love Me, keep My commandments."

Jesus wants to write His law on our hearts, empowering us by His grace. No matter our past failures, He invites us to a new beginning with Him today.

Final Reflection:

  • Is it your desire today to ask Jesus to write His law on your heart and empower you to live by His principles out of love for Him?
