How to Use This Study Guide

This guide is designed to help you delve deeper into the themes presented in the sermon "Voices From Beyond The Grave." As you go through each section:

  • Read the summary points and key scriptures.
  • Reflect on the questions provided. Consider writing down your thoughts or discussing them with others.
  • Note the "Key Takeaways" which highlight crucial biblical principles.
  • Pray for understanding and wisdom as you study God's Word.
  • Consider listening to the accompanying podcast audio using the player above for a richer experience.

Introduction: The Search for Answers

This study guide accompanies the sermon "Voices From Beyond The Grave." The sermon addresses the common fascination with communicating with the dead and the dangers of spiritual deception. It emphasizes that our eternal salvation can be at stake, making it crucial to find solid answers based on a reliable source: God's Word, the Bible.

Key Question from Sermon: How can we know for absolute certainty whether supposed communications with the dead are genuine, unreal, or false and dangerous?

1. The Bible's Clear Warning Against Spiritism

The sermon highlights that the Bible uniquely and strongly condemns spiritism and any attempts to communicate with the dead. Such practices are called an "abomination" to the Lord.

Key Scriptures:

  • Leviticus 19:31:
    "Give no regard to mediums and familiar spirits; do not seek after them, to be defiled by them: I am the Lord your God."
  • Deuteronomy 18:10-12:
    "There shall not be found among you anyone who... practices witchcraft, or a soothsayer, or one who interprets omens, or a sorcerer, or one who conjures spells, or a medium, or a spiritist, or one who calls up the dead. For all who do these things are an abomination to the Lord..."
  • (Reference to Exodus 22:18 mentioned as: "You shall not permit a sorcerer or a witch to live.")

Reflection:

  • Why do you think God gives such strong warnings against these practices?
  • The sermon mentions King Saul's experience. How does his story (see 1 Samuel 28) illustrate the folly of seeking guidance from mediums?

2. What the Bible Teaches About Death

A core teaching is that communication with the dead is prohibited because it is, according to the Bible, impossible. Jesus Christ holds the keys to death and Hades and is the ultimate authority on life and death.

Key Scriptures:

  • Revelation 1:17-18:
    "...I am the First and the Last. I am He who lives, and was dead, and behold, I am alive forevermore. Amen. And I have the keys of Hades and of Death."
  • Job 7:9-10:
    "As the cloud disappears and vanishes away, So he who goes down to the grave does not come up. He shall never return to his house, Nor shall his place know him anymore."
  • Ecclesiastes 9:5:
    "For the living know that they will die; But the dead know nothing, And they have no more reward, For the memory of them is forgotten."
Key Takeaway:The Bible teaches that the dead are not conscious and do not communicate with the living. They "know nothing."

Reflection:

  • How do these verses challenge popular ideas about ghosts or spirits of the deceased interacting with the living?

3. If Not the Dead, Then Who? The Source of "Spiritual" Communications

If the dead are unconscious, then who or what are the entities that people claim to communicate with through mediums or psychics? The Bible identifies them as demonic spirits (fallen angels).

Key Scriptures:

  • Revelation 16:13-14:
    "And I saw three unclean spirits like frogs coming out of the mouth of the dragon... For they are spirits of demons, performing signs..."
  • Revelation 12:7-9:
    "And war broke out in heaven... So the great dragon was cast out, that serpent of old, called the Devil and Satan, who deceives the whole world; he was cast to the earth, and his angels were cast out with him."
  • 2 Corinthians 11:14:
    "And no wonder! For Satan himself transforms himself into an angel of light."
Key Takeaway:Entities claiming to be spirits of the dead are, according to the Bible, demons in disguise, led by Satan, with the aim to deceive.

Reflection:

  • The sermon states these demons can "impersonate perfectly every person who has ever lived." Why is this ability so dangerous?
  • How does Satan appearing as an "angel of light" make his deceptions even more effective?

4. Satan's Ultimate Deceptions

The deception can go beyond impersonating loved ones. The Bible warns that Satan will even seek to impersonate Christ himself, performing great signs and wonders.

Key Scripture:

  • Matthew 24:23-25:
    "Then if anyone says to you, ‘Look, here is the Christ!’ or ‘There!’ do not believe it. For false christs and false prophets will rise and show great signs and wonders to deceive, if possible, even the elect. See, I have told you beforehand."
Key Takeaway:Our only safety from profound deception is to base our beliefs firmly on what God's Word teaches, even if it contradicts our senses or feelings.

Reflection:

  • What does it mean to "fortify their minds with the words of the Bible"? How can one do this practically?
  • Discuss the challenge of relying on the Bible over personal experiences or feelings, especially when those experiences seem very real or comforting.

5. Addressing Near-Death Experiences (NDEs) and Reincarnation

Near-Death Experiences:

The sermon acknowledges reports of NDEs but suggests they can have natural explanations (medication, oxygen deprivation) and, critically, that they cannot be reconciled with the Bible's teachings about the state of the dead.

Reincarnation:

The teaching of reincarnation (prominent in Hinduism and Buddhism) is contrasted with biblical truth. Reincarnation involves cycles of rebirth, karma, and an ultimate goal of nirvana (a state of nothingness). The Bible, instead, speaks of a single life, judgment, and a personal, conscious eternal life with God.

Key Scriptures (for contrast):
  • John 10:10b:
    "I have come that they may have life, and that they may have it more abundantly."
    (Contrast to nirvana)
  • 1 Corinthians 13:12:
    "For now we see in a mirror, dimly, but then face to face. Now I know in part, but then I shall know just as I also am known."
    (Describing a personal, enhanced future existence)

Reflection:

  • Why is it important, according to the sermon, to evaluate all spiritual experiences and beliefs through the lens of Scripture?
  • How does the biblical concept of a personal, abundant eternal life differ from the concept of nirvana?

6. The Christian's Hope: Resurrection and a New Earth

The Bible offers a powerful and hopeful alternative to spiritual confusion and the finality of death as commonly perceived. It speaks of resurrection and a future new heaven and new earth, free from sin, suffering, and death.

Key Scriptures:

  • Revelation 21:1, 3-4:
    "Now I saw a new heaven and a new earth... ‘Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men... And God will wipe away every tear from their eyes; there shall be no more death, nor sorrow, nor crying. There shall be no more pain, for the former things have passed away.’"
  • 1 Peter 1:3-4:
    "Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who according to His abundant mercy has begotten us again to a living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead, to an inheritance incorruptible and undefiled and that does not fade away, reserved in heaven for you."
Key Takeaway:The ultimate hope for believers is not in contacting spirits but in the resurrection to eternal life in God's renewed creation.

7. Securing Eternal Life: The Path to God's Kingdom

Access to this future hope is through being "born again" by faith in Jesus Christ, which can happen now, today.

Key Scriptures:

  • 1 John 5:11-13:
    "And this is the testimony: that God has given us eternal life, and this life is in His Son. He who has the Son has life; he who does not have the Son of God does not have life. These things I have written to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, that you may know that you have eternal life."
  • Revelation 3:20:
    "Behold, I stand at the door and knock. If anyone hears My voice and opens the door, I will come in to him and dine with him, and he with Me."

Reflection:

  • According to these verses, what is the condition for receiving eternal life?
  • The sermon emphasizes that "there is one voice beyond the grave that is speaking to us... the voice of Jesus." How does one hear and respond to this voice today?

Conclusion: Choosing Truth Over Deception

The central message urges listeners to trust the voice of Jesus as revealed in the Bible, rather than deceptive spiritual experiences or popular misconceptions about death. By opening our hearts to Christ, we can find true comfort, guidance, and the assurance of eternal life.

Final Thought:

Are you fortifying your mind with the Word of God to stand against deception? Will you choose to listen to the voice of Jesus today?
